Matthias Meng Yan Wong

Postdoctoral Research Assistant, Department of History, University of Hull
Senior Tutor, Department of History, National University of Singapore

I am a historian of early modern mental worlds, with interests in understanding senses of time and place, and the repercussions of trauma and disruption. My doctoral research at the University of Cambridge, conducted under the supervision of Dr David Smith examined the execution of King Charles I as a moment of ideational change and adaptation. Taking a diachronic approach to periodicals like almanacs and newsbooks, I traced how ideas of time and the future evolved in response to unexpected political change.

As a Postdoctoral Research Assistant with the ‘Brightening the Covenant Chain’ AHRC project, I will be visualising concepts of space and movement in the form of a ‘kinetic map’. This digital platform, created in collaboration with King’s Digital Lab, is an exciting opportunity to communicate historical research and sources in a new visual manner. The project may illustrate previously unseen connections in the documentary record, and it also has the potential to reach a wide audience and communities traditionally excluded from the conversation.

I completed my MSc at the S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies, a graduate school of Nanyang Technological University, where I focused on aspects of discourse analysis and the role of imagined spaces in the domain of international law and diplomacy. My first degree was in History, jointly awarded by the National University of Singapore and the Australian National University, where I first started working on early modern England.

I am an Associate Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (AFHEA) and an Early Career Researcher member of the Royal Historical Society.
